# Graded — Brand Definition

*The public record for sports picks.*

---

## 1. Who we're for

### The Tailer

The person Graded serves is the everyday consumer of sports betting content —
the community's own word for them is a **tailer** (to "tail" a pick is to copy it).

**Who they are.** 21–34, came of age after sports betting went legal state-by-state.
Bets recreationally on DraftKings or FanDuel — $10 to $50 a bet, more on a big
Sunday. Sports is social currency: the group chat, the slip screenshot, the sweat
during the fourth quarter.

**What they do.** They follow 5–20 pick accounts ("cappers") across X, Instagram,
Telegram, and Reddit. They tail picks. They screenshot slips. They know the
vocabulary — units, juice, ROI, fade, chalk — and they use it correctly.

**The wound.** They've been burned, or watched a friend get burned. The capper
economy runs on fabricated records:

- Losing picks get deleted; only winners get the victory-lap screenshot.
- "30-12 last month 🔥" — with no way to check.
- Free picks are a funnel to a $50/month Discord, sold off a record no one audited.

**Their learned behavior.** Distrust — but effortful, manual distrust. They
screenshot cappers' posts *themselves* so there's proof later. They read the reply
guys who catch deletions. They ask "post your full record" and never get it.

**The job they'd hire Graded for:**

1. *"Before I tail or pay anyone, tell me if they're actually good."*
2. *"Catch the ones who delete."*
3. *"Give me the receipt that settles the argument."*

### The insight

> The audience for betting picks isn't gullible — they're **unverifiable**. They
> already distrust cappers; what they lack isn't skepticism, it's *evidence*.
> Nobody in the ecosystem is neutral: cappers grade themselves, and betting media
> is paid by sportsbooks. The tailer has no referee.

**Graded is the referee.**

## 2. Positioning

**For** people who follow sports betting picks,
**Graded is** the independent public record
**that** captures every publicly posted pick, grades it against the final score,
and keeps it on the record forever — including the ones cappers delete.
**Unlike** capper-run records and paid Discords,
**Graded** sells nothing, tips nothing, and can't be edited after the fact.

**Tagline:** *Every pick tracked. Every deletion counted.*

What Graded is **not** (as load-bearing as what it is):

- Not a tout. Graded never recommends a pick.
- Not a sportsbook affiliate. No odds boosts, no signup links.
- Not a hype machine. A hot streak gets the same font size as a cold one.

---

## 3. Personality & voice

**The referee.** Neutral, terse, unimpressed. Whistles the play, states the call,
moves on. The refusal to hype *is* the brand — every competitor screams; the
credible one doesn't.

| Voice rule | Instead of… | Graded says… |
|---|---|---|
| Short declaratives | "He's been absolutely on fire lately!!" | "W7 streak. 41-38-2 on the season." |
| Numbers carry the emotion | "🔥🔥 HUGE win" | "+4.2u last night." |
| Losses are losses | "Tough beat 😤" | "Loss. −1.10u." |
| Never sells | "Tail him now before the line moves!" | "Record below. Decide for yourself." |
| Deletion is stated, not mocked | "Caught this coward deleting 🤡" | "Deleted after loss. Still counted." |

No emojis. No exclamation points. No lock icons. The reader brings the emotion;
Graded brings the arithmetic.

## 4. Visual identity

**Concept: the scoreboard, not the sportsbook.** Sportsbook design is a casino —
saturated gradients, confetti, urgency. Graded is the opposite surface: the black
scoreboard that hangs *above* the noise and just shows the numbers.

### Color

Near-monochrome. The referee doesn't wear team colors — color is reserved for
*meaning*.

| Token | Value | Role |
|---|---|---|
| Court Black | `#0a0a0a` (neutral-950) | Background, everywhere |
| Board Charcoal | `#171717` (neutral-900) | Cards, raised surfaces |
| Hairline | `#262626` (neutral-800) | Borders, rules |
| Chalk | `#ededed` (neutral-100/200) | Primary ink |
| Steel | `#a3a3a3` (neutral-400/500) | Secondary ink, labels |
| Win Green | `#4ade80` (green-400) | Positive units, wins — **only** |
| Loss Red | `#f87171` (red-400) | Negative units, losses — **only** |
| Flag Amber | `#fbbf24` (amber-400) | Integrity flags: deletions, late posts, edits — **only** |

Signal colors are never decorative. Green means profit, red means loss, amber
means "a capper tried something." If it's green and it isn't money, it's wrong.

### Typography

| Face | Role | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Barlow Condensed** (600/700, caps) | Display: wordmark, page titles, big numbers | Jersey numbers, ticker boards, stadium signage |
| **Geist Sans** | Body, UI | Neutral, modern, disappears |
| **Tabular numerals** (everywhere numbers appear) | Records, units, odds | Columns align like a box score; numbers are the product |

### Motifs

1. **The grade stamp.** Cappers earn a letter grade (A+ … F) from ROI once they
   have 20 graded picks — a boxed chip beside the name. The product is named
   Graded; the report card is the brand mark in action.
2. **The box score.** Hairline-ruled rows, dense and quiet. Data tables aren't a
   utility to hide — they're the aesthetic.
3. **The strikethrough.** A deleted pick renders struck through, in amber, still
   counted in the math. The flagship visual gesture: *you can delete the post, not
   the record.*

### Layout

Dense, data-forward, generous black space between sections. Small radii, hairline
borders. No stock photography, no illustrations of people, no confetti. If a
surface needs decorating, put a number on it.

---

## 5. Product principles (brand, applied)

1. **Empty states tell the truth.** "Records are building" beats fake seed data.
   A tracker you can trust never shows a number it didn't compute.
2. **Flags over bans.** Suspicious behavior is displayed, not silently filtered —
   the reader judges.
3. **Reproducibility is a feature.** Every stat traces to an archived post; the
   methodology page is the whole rulebook, public.
4. **The house voice never bets.** No pick of the day, ever.
